Urban Climbing

I like to explore places with extraordinary views. These include public places like observatories, as well as hidden places like rooftops, construction sites, etc.

Here’s a list of public skyscrapers / observatories I’ve set foot on:

  • 2021: The John Hancock Center, Chicago (344m)

  • 2020: U.S. Bank Tower, Los Angeles (310 m)

  • 2018: Eiffel Tower, Paris (276 m)

  • 2018: Arc de Triomphe, Paris (50 m)

  • 2018: Taipei 101, Taipei (439 m)

  • 2018: The Shanghai Tower, Shanghai (632 m)

  • 2017: Rockefeller Center, New York City (266 m)

  • 2017: Columbia Center, Seattle (284 m)

  • 2017: Shun Hing Square, Shenzhen (384 m)

  • 2017: Beijing Olympic Tower, Beijing (258 m)

  • 2016: The Shard, London (306 m)

  • 2016: 20 Fenchurch Street, London (160 m)

  • 2016: Etihad Tower 2, Abu Dhabi (305 m)

  • 2016: Capital Gate, Abu Dhabi (160 m)

  • 2016: Burj Khalifa, Dubai (828 m)

  • 2016: JW Marriott Marquis Dubai, Dubai (355 m)

  • 2016: Tianjin Radio and Television Tower, Tianjin (415 m)

  • 2015: Canton Tower, Guangzhou (604 m)

  • 2015 & 2021: Willis Tower, Chicago (413 m)

  • 2015 & 2017: Space Needle, Seattle (158 m)

  • 2015: Empire States Building, New York City (381 m)

  • 2012: Dragon Tower, Harbin (216 m)

  • 2010: Shanghai World Financial Center, Shanghai (492 m)

  • 2009: Qingdao TV Tower, Qingdao (232 m)

  • 2006: Sky Tower, Auckland (328 m)
